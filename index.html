Research interests

Evolutionary Computation

Evolutionary Computation is a computational method based on the principles of biological evolution for solving complex problems and optimization tasks. It origins from Darwin's theory of evolution, simulates the genetic and evolutionary processes in nature, and searches for excellent solutions in the solution space through generation-by-generation evolution and selection, including Genetic Algorithms (GA), Evolution Strategies (ES), Genetic Programming (GP) and so on.

Natural Language Processing

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is the study of how to enable computers to understand and process natural human language. It involves the development and application of a range of algorithms and techniques that enable computers to extract meaningful information from text or speech data, and to perform tasks such as text comprehension, language generation, machine translation, sentiment analysis, question-and-answer systems, and text classification.

Online Learning

Consequences that data overview is hidden and arrives in a stream. The model must adapt to the data distribution change. The online learning approach allows the model to learn and update incrementally as it receives new data. It can adapt to changes in data distribution, process new samples and adjust model parameters to fit new contexts and needs.

Memrister Computing

Memristor Computing is a computational model and calculation method based on the technology of Memristor, an electronic component with nonlinear resistance characteristics, whose resistance value can be adjusted according to the current and voltage passing through it, and can be maintained in the adjusted state, i.e., it has memory.
